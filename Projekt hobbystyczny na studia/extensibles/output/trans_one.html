Article Two

Several anime studios are widely recognized and well-known in the anime industry for their contributions to the medium. As of my last knowledge update in January 2022, here are some of the most well-known anime studios:

  • Studio Ghibli: Renowned for its enchanting and critically acclaimed films, including "Spirited Away," "My Neighbor Totoro," and "Princess Mononoke."
  • Toei Animation: A major animation studio with a long history, known for producing popular and long-running series such as "Dragon Ball," "One Piece," and "Sailor Moon."
  • Kyoto Animation (KyoAni): Recognized for its high-quality animation and emotionally resonant stories, with works like "Clannad," "K-On!," and "A Silent Voice."
  • Madhouse: Known for its diverse range of anime genres and notable series like "Death Note," "One Punch Man" (season 1), and "Hunter x Hunter (2011)."
  • Bones: Notable for its dynamic animation and involvement in series such as "Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood," "My Hero Academia," and "Mob Psycho 100."
  • A-1 Pictures: Involved in a variety of anime genres, with series like "Sword Art Online," "Blue Exorcist," and "Anohana."
  • Ufotable: Gained recognition for its exceptional animation quality, particularly in the "Fate" series ("Fate/Zero," "Fate/stay night: Unlimited Blade Works") and "Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba."
  • Wit Studio: Known for its contribution to the production of "Attack on Titan."
  • Sunrise: A long-standing studio with a history of mecha anime, responsible for the "Gundam" series, "Code Geass," and more.
  • Shaft: Noted for its unique visual style and involvement in series like the "Monogatari Series," "Puella Magi Madoka Magica," and "March Comes in Like a Lion."

    • These studios have played significant roles in shaping the anime industry, and their works are often celebrated by fans worldwide. It's important to note that the popularity and recognition of studios can evolve, and new studios may gain prominence over time.
